# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dual audience: internal Mojo's staff and managers who maintain brand consistency across 4 Thailand locations, and external creative partners (designers, printers, uniform makers, fit-out contractors, agencies) who need a definitive reference. Both groups need clarity fast — internal ops want quick answers, external suppliers need unambiguous specs.

## Product Purpose

An interactive branding guidelines document for Mojo's Fire & Smoke, a bold American comfort food restaurant on Koh Phangan and Koh Samui, Thailand. The site IS the brand system — it communicates identity, color, typography, logo, tableware, uniforms, music, menu direction, and location context in one scrollable reference. Success means anyone who reads it can reproduce the Mojo's aesthetic without a briefing call.

## Brand Personality

Bold. Unapologetic. Loud.

Rock-and-roll energy meets American smokehouse. Confident, a little rowdy, but dead serious about the food. "All Flavour. No Fuss." — the personality is in the food, not the attitude. Est. 2026. Fire & Smoke. David Bowie on the wall, matte black plates on the table.

## Anti-references

- Corporate fast food (McDonald's, KFC) — bright primaries, plastic-happy, family-safe
- Generic food delivery apps (Uber Eats, Grab) — functional beige, safe, transactional UI
- Hipster craft / artisan — hand-lettered, earth tones, mason jars, "small batch" preciousness
- Fine dining / white tablecloth — minimal, precious, hushed — wrong energy entirely

## Design Principles

1. **The food is the hero.** Every design decision serves the food and the feeling, not the interface.
2. **Confidence over cleverness.** Bold choices executed cleanly beat intricate flourishes executed tentatively.
3. **Dark is the default.** Charcoal and crimson are not a style choice — they are the brand. Never lighten toward safety.
4. **Rock culture, not theme park.** Music references feel lived-in, not decorative. The poster wall is real.
5. **Specs that actually ship.** The guidelines exist to be used. Ambiguity is failure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Primary concern: contrast ratios on dark backgrounds with crimson red text/accents — this is the highest-risk combination in the palette. Keyboard navigation required. No formal compliance mandate but AA is the working standard.
